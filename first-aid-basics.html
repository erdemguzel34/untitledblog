The Three P's
  1. Preserve Life (Zarardan koru)
  2. Prevent further injury (Yeni yaraları engelle)
  3. Promote recovery (İyileşmeyi teşvik et)

When someone injured, it's all-too-easy to panic and forget what you need to do provide assistance The "Three P's" reminds what you need to do at this point.

Check the scene for danger

Before you rush to help someone, take a moment to analyse the area and spot anything that could injure you.
Before you go runing outside to help them, look for hazards.

Treating Cuts and Scrapes

When someone is bleeding, you want to prevent as much blood from leaving their body as possible.
Try and find clean cloth or bandage.
Then:
Apply gently pressure for 20 to 30 minutes.
Clean the wound by gently runnin clean water over it. Avoid using soap on an open wound!
Apply antibiotic to the wound, like Neosprin.
Cover the wound with bandage.
The body is usually very quick at patching up small cuts and scrapes but deeper wounds may require medical attention.
Whit deep bounds:
Don't apply ointments(merhemler işe yaramaz)
Cover the are with loose cloth to prevent contaminants from infecting the wound.
Seek medical attention as soon as possible.

Treating Sprains(Burkulma tedavisi)

Sprains are usally an unalarming injury and most of the time they'll heal on their own. Swelling is caused by flow to an injured area. You can reduce swelling by applying ice. Ice restricts the blood vessels, which reduces blood flow.
Keep the injuring limb elevated.
Apply ice to the injuring area. Don't apply ice directly to the skin. Wrap it in a cloth or put ice in a plastic bay.
Keep the injuring area compressed. Put it in a brace or tightly wrap it. Don't wrap it so tight it cut off circulation.
Ice for a while. Then compress. Repeat at intervals.
Make sure the injured person avoids putting weight on the injured limb.
